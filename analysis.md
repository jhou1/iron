## What's Going Well

### LC at 32kg is on a strong upward curve

From Feb to April 2026, session reps climbed: 64 -> 70 -> 87 -> 78 -> 92 -> 83 -> 96. The April 2 session (96 reps, 3072kg volume) was the best 32kg session ever. Notes confirm: "动作变流畅了" and "成绩有提高".

### Snatch technique has matured

Progressed from struggling with 24kg in 2024 to 223 reps in 10 min at 16kg (Mar 14, 2026), and comfortably handling 20-24kg. Specific weaknesses identified and worked on (left shoulder stability, trunk rigidity, hip power).

### Meels has been a steady climb

From struggling with 6.7kg in 2024 to hitting the 100-hand milestone in May 2025. Now consistently at 13.4kg with improving technique. Notes show deepening movement understanding.

This is the biggest red flag. 5+ incidents in 2 years:

| Date | Incident |

| Feb 2024 | Right lower back pain from snatch |

Root cause is consistent: insufficient warmup, or pushing weight/volume when the body isn't ready.

### 2. The 40kg Jump Was Premature

Peak form at 32kg was reached on Apr 2 (96 reps). Then Apr 8 jumped to 40kg (56 reps), and Apr 15 got hurt at 40kg (only 25 reps before stopping). 32kg was becoming comfortable but not fully adapted. The injury confirmed the timing was off.

## The Core Issue

The day after a hard LC or Snatch session, the body feels tense and not ready for heavy training. But training often happens anyway. Over 2 years this has led to 5+ back injuries. For a 40-year training horizon, this is the #1 thing to fix.

The best LC performance (96 reps on Apr 2) came after a period of reduced volume. The note says it all: "最近一直没有上量，所以身体恢复得不错，后退一点点，前进一大步."

## Recommendations

### Recommended Weekly Structure (5 Days)

The key principle: never do two high-CNS days back-to-back. Alternate heavy and light days.

| Day | Focus | Intensity |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (Mon) | **LC** -- main lift | Hard |
| Day 2 (Tue) | **Meels + Shena** | Light |
| Day 3 (Wed) | **Snatch** -- second main lift | Hard |
| Day 4 (Thu) | **Meels + Shena + Accessory** (SLDL, TGU) | Light |
| Day 5 (Fri/Sat) | **Mace or Variety** -- movement practice | Light |

This gives 2 hard days and 3 light days, with at least 72 hours between LC and Snatch. The body gets the recovery it needs.

### 1. Stay at 32kg LC for at Least 8 More Weeks

The upward curve at 32kg was strong. Go back to it. The Apr 2 session (96 reps) showed growth isn't done at this weight. Aim for consistent 90-100 rep sessions before considering 40kg. When moving to 40kg, start at 50% of 32kg volume (~45-50 reps) and build from there.

### 2. Add Back Accessory Work

On light days (Day 2 and Day 4):

- **KB Single Leg Dead Lift** -- directly protects the back (was done regularly in 2025)
- **Turkish Get-Up** on the left side -- identified as needed on Feb 26 but never started
- **KB Swing** at moderate weight -- builds posterior chain that supports LC

These don't tax the CNS much but build the structural support the main lifts need.

### 3. Mandatory Warmup Protocol Before Every Heavy Session

Back injuries share one cause: "热身不充分." Non-negotiable checklist:

1. Foam roller
2. Dynamic stretches (hip circles, thoracic rotation)
3. 2-3 min of light swings (16kg)
4. 1 light set of the main movement before working weight

### 4. Respect the HRV -- Make It a Rule, Not a Suggestion

Simple rule: **if HRV says low, do a light day regardless of schedule.** Swap the hard day to later in the week, or skip it entirely. One missed session costs nothing. One back injury costs 2-4 weeks.

### 5. Snatch: Stay at 20kg as the Training Weight

Recent sessions at 20kg (Apr 16: 113 reps) show this is the productive training zone. 20kg allows volume accumulation and GS technique refinement without destroying hands or shoulders. Use 24kg occasionally to test, not as the daily weight.

---

## The Bottom Line

This is not a volume problem -- it's a **recovery management problem**. The body is strong enough. It has shown it can do 96 reps of LC at 32kg and 223 snatches at 16kg. What it has also shown, through 5 back injuries and repeated "过训" notes, is that it needs more structured rest between hard efforts.

For 40 years of training, the formula is: **train hard, recover fully, then train hard again.**

后退一点点，前进一大步。
